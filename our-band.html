Born in the depths of a Secondary School Maths class, The Jones' started out as five young teens with mediocre experience and limited enthusiasm.
Today they are "That band that plays the songs you know, but forgot that you knew". And are down to 4 members.

Equally well-equipped to play weddings, debs balls and Bar Mitzvahs, The Jones' have played for as many as some thousand people in the likes of Vicar St.,
to the 7 or so who showed up that night in Kells (cheers lads - the Bus is on its way).
Likely to shout various catchphrases from the stage ("Sharon!", "Must be the Money").

Twice runners up at the Jacobs Song Contest, the lads developed a serious addiction to chocolate Kimberley biscuits at said competition but were privilieged to meet the late Gerry Ryan and even got to rub strings with now-disappeared The Revs.
